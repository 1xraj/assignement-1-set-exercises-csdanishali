Breaking Bad

Breaking Bad is a renowned American crime drama television series conceived by Vince Gilligan. It aired on the AMC network from January 20, 2008, through September 29, 2013.

The narrative follows Walter White, a high school chemistry teacher, whose life spirals into the criminal underworld as he becomes a drug kingpin.

The series garnered high praise and multiple accolades, including 16 Primetime Emmy Awards.

What Critics Say

The series has been universally acclaimed and is widely regarded as one of the greatest television dramas ever created.

According to Rotten Tomatoes, it holds a 98% approval score based on 87 reviews, with an average rating of 9.4/10. The consensus states: "Breaking Bad is a gripping portrayal of a man driven to the edge by financial desperation and moral decay."
